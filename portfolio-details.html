LPG Monitoring System

IoT Project

This project presented an early warning detection system that comprises a remotely routed integrated circuit with sensors for detecting LP gas leakages at filling station. The information gathered from this detection is channeled to a web application via the Internet so that the Station Owners as well as the Regulated Body - the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) can take safety measures to protect both human lives and property.

Motivation

On October 8, 2017, Ghanaian News Outlet JoyOnline published a piece on its website describing the horrifying event of a fire explosion that shook the entire city of Accra. The fire explosion which caused the death of over 7 people and injured a little over 130 others, was caused by an explosion of gas (LPG) at nearby petrol filling station. This incident, which was caused by the discharge of LPG by an untrained driverâ€™s mate, occurred on October 7, 2017 at the MANSCO gas station at Atomic Junction, Accra. This incident caused a stir in the media as to whether the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) is doing its job well. In 2016, there was also another new publication of gas explosion by GhanaWeb which happened near the International Trade Fair Center at La, Accra. This gas explosion incident led to the loss of nine lives and 12 others injured. In the light of this unfortunate incident, I would like to embark on this topic to experiment the viability and progress of Internet of Things (IoT) in the field of detecting early signs of gas leakages to prevent explosion. This project is pursued because it will significantly attach greater credibility to my practical knowledge as a computer engineer and further raise awareness for maximum attention to be given to sudden massive gas leakages that leads to explosion at gas filling stations in Ghana.
